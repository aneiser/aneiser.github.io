Standalone HERA

HERA is a semi-automatic accessibility evaluation tool originated from the Firefox plugin HERA-FFX

Hera-FFX usability problems

What made a tool that presents many of the features desired by web accessibility consultants that use it was a tedious job? The answer was clear. A usability well below average and clearly improveable.

But wait, how do I knew that? Well, I got to work! I conducted an usability problems analysis

This analysis consisted of three parts:

  • A System Usability Scale Satisfaction Survey
  • An AttrakDiff Survey
  • Coments from users

System Usability Scale Satisfaction Survey

10 statements. 5 positives and 5 negatives to which the user must respond on a scale from 1 to 5 what is his o her level of agreement with each sentence. This results in a score

The average score of more than 500 SUS studies is 68. Hera-FFX scored 53,5. According to the percentile range Hera-FFX did not reach a D. Which means it failed the survey.

SUS chart ALT

AttrakDiff Survey

28 pairs of opposing concepts valued as positive or negative. Complicated Vs. Simple, Ugly vs. Attractive, etc.

The user indicates which concept is closer to his or her perception and how close it is.

Half of the concepts were positives and the other half negatives.

Comments from users

The comments were collected through free response questions. In general those were negatives.

It takes a too much time, To much clicks are needed, the UI is ugly and obsolete, the tools is not friendly are some examples.

Only one user, among 57 young europeans, said positives comments.

As a result, a series of improvements proposals were made. Those proposals were focus on efficiency, automatisms, accessibility, help and feedback and graphic design.

User tests

The user tests were conducted by one facilitator and an observer.

In the test we studied the interaction of 5 people with the low fidelity prototype. 3 of them were acessibility experts and 2 novices students.

We got ready the tasks list, the surveys, the observer sheet and we even made an evaluation protocol. Oh! there was a pilot test too.

Get to the point. Results

I'm going to keep the same structure in order to facilitate the comparison. Let's recap:

  • The System Usability Scale Satisfaction Survey
  • The AttrakDiff Survey
  • The coments from users

System Usability Scale Satisfaction Survey

The Hera-FFX average scored was 53,5. Hera standalone got an average of 65,6.

This mean 7,5 points more, an improvement of 12% in the percentil range. Now it get slightly over C, which mean Standalone Hera pass it, and almost reach the SUS average.

But let's calm down. It is needed to realize that these surveys were made with a considerable smaller sample. It is less representative. Also, we are comparing a prototype with closed task and less time of use with a real tool with real taks and more, more time of use.

AttrakDiff Survey

Remember pairs of opposing concepts valued as positive or negative, in which Hera-FFX obtanined a 50/50.

With the low fidelity prototype these figures changed up to 86% positive concepts, 10% negative concepts and 4% of neutral concepts.
