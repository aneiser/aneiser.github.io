Standalone HERA

HERA is a semi-automatic accessibility evaluation tool originated from the Firefox plugin HERA-FFX

Hera-FFX usability problems

What made a tool that presents many of the features desired by web accessibility consultants that use it was a tedious job? The answer was clear. A usability well below average and clearly improveable.

But wait, how do I knew that? Well, I got to work! I conducted an usability problems analysis

This analysis consisted of three parts:

  • A System Usability Scale Satisfaction Survey
  • An AttrakDiff Survey
  • Coments from users

A System Usability Scale Satisfaction Survey

10 statements. 5 positives and 5 negatives to which the user must respond on a scale from 1 to 5 what is his o her level of agreement with each sentence. This results in a score

The average SUS Score between different webs and tools is 68. Hera scored 53,5. According to the percentile range Hera did not reach a D. Which means it failed the survey.

An AttrakDiff Survey

28 pairs of opposing concepts. Complicated Vs. Simple, Ugly vs. Attractive, etc.

The user indicates which concept is closer to his or her perception and how close it is.

Half of the answers were positives and the other half negatives.

Comments from users

The comments were collected through free response questions. In general those were negatives.

It takes a too much time, To much clicks are needed, the UI is ugly and obsolete, the tools is not friendly are some examples.

Only one user, among, 57 said positives comments.

As a result, a series of improvements proposals were made. Those proposals were focus on efficiency, automatisms, accessibility, help and feedback and graphic design.
